Problem Statement

The innovative approach of leveraging nanotechnology to create biodegradable superabsorbent polymers (SAPs) from natural sources, such as orange peels and chitosan from shrimp shells, presents a promising solution to address water scarcity in Indian agriculture.

Proposed Solution

The solution of creating a low-cost, biodegradable superabsorbent polymer (SAP) from orange peels presents a groundbreaking approach to address water conservation and food security challenges in India.

Method

Step Description
Step 1 Peel three full oranges, add lemon juice, and leave for 1 hour.
Step 2 Boil the peels with water, dry them, and combine with avocado skin.
Step 3 Dry the mixture for 14 days, then crush into powder.

Conclusion

Superabsorbent polymers improve soil water retention. The orange peel SAP offers an eco-friendly, cost-effective solution that benefits agriculture in drought-prone areas.
